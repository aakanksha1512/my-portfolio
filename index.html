About Me

I'm a software engineer specializing in distributed backend systems and cloud-native infrastructure, currently building production systems at Onsemi that process hundreds of thousands of events daily. My core stack is Java Spring Boot and Python on AWS — Kafka, Redis, Kubernetes, and Terraform are daily tools, not buzzwords.

I care deeply about observability, reliability, and the engineering culture around them — the kind of systems that you can sleep through an on-call rotation because you built them right. I've reduced MTTR from 45 to 12 minutes and compressed release cycles from 2 weeks to 3 days; those numbers come from treating infrastructure as a product, not an afterthought.

I'm increasingly working at the backend-AI intersection — RAG pipelines, LangChain agents, vector databases, LLM inference endpoints. This is where the most interesting system design problems live right now, and it's the area I'm investing in most deeply. My churn prediction platform and sticky notes projects reflect that bet.

I hold an MS in Software Engineering from Arizona State University (3.8 GPA) and an AWS Solutions Architect Associate certification, and I'm completing the IBM Data Science Specialization. I'm based in the NYC/NJ metro area, open to backend SWE, platform engineering, data engineering, and AI engineering roles.
